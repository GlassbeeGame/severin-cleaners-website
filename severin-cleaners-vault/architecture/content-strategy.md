## Content Voice

This is a local San Diego trades business. Write like a knowledgeable local who does this work every day, not a content agency.

### Do

- Name actual streets, landmarks, zip codes, neighborhoods
- Use specific numbers: "$249 quarter-load" not "affordable pricing"
- Write short, direct sentences
- Sound like someone explaining the service over the phone
- Reference real San Diego geography: "up the hill from Grossmont Center" or "off the 8 freeway"
- Use "we" and "our team" naturally

### Don't

- Use AI-isms: "delve," "comprehensive," "leverage," "streamline," "navigate," "landscape," "utilize," "facilitate," "in today's world"
- Write in generic marketing speak: "We are committed to providing exceptional service"
- Use filler paragraphs that say nothing specific
- Describe services the business doesn't offer
- Write the same paragraph in different words across multiple pages
- Use passive voice when active is clearer

### Service Scope — Hard Boundaries

**Severin Hauling offers**: Junk removal and hauling. Period.

**Never mention or describe**:
- Cleaning services
- Moving services
- Organizing services
- Airbnb/vacation rental turnovers
- Equestrian specialists
- Property management services
- Any service not on the list in `architecture/service-pages.md`

## Keyword Optimization Rules

### Density & Placement

- Overall keyword density: 1.0-1.3% for primary keyword
- Primary keyword: 8-12 exact-match mentions per page
- Secondary keyword: 5-8 exact-match mentions per page

### Strategic Placement (in priority order)

1. H1 tag
2. First paragraph (first 100 words)
3. At least one H2
4. 1-2 H3s
5. At least one FAQ question
6. Meta title and description

### Readability Rules

- Use natural phrasing — never force keywords where they sound awkward
- After establishing context, use pronouns: "the area," "downtown," "locally," "the city"
- Vary keyword forms: "junk removal in La Jolla," "La Jolla junk hauling," "removing junk from La Jolla homes"
- If it reads wrong out loud, rewrite it

## Heading Hierarchy

Every page must follow proper heading hierarchy:

- **One H1 per page** — the main page title
- **H2s** for major sections (3-8 per page depending on content length)
- **H3s** for subsections under H2s
- **Never skip levels** — don't jump from H2 to H4

## Word Count Targets by Page Type

| Page Type | Target | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Location pages | 2,000-2,500 | Scannable, conversion-focused. NOT 5,000+ |
| Service pages | 2,000-3,000 | Service-focused depth |
| Blog posts | 1,500-2,500 | Comprehensive guides |
| Hub pages | 500-1,000 | Navigation-focused |

### Why Location Pages Are Shorter Than They Used to Be

Original location pages were 5,000-6,200 words. The optimization template (`docs/templates/LOCATION_PAGE_OPTIMIZATION_TEMPLATE.md`) established that cutting to 2,000-2,500 words while keeping location-specific value improves readability, reduces bounce rate, and maintains ranking. Generic filler content (traffic patterns, DIY comparisons, repetitive explanations) was removed.

## Content Quality Checklist

Run through this for every piece of content:

- [ ] Helpful content first — provides genuine value, not just keywords
- [ ] Natural keyword integration — no stuffing, reads like a human wrote it
- [ ] Accurate local information — neighborhoods, landmarks, weather are real and current
- [ ] Service scope accuracy — only mentions services actually offered
- [ ] Scannable format — headers, bullets, bold text for key terms
- [ ] Mobile-friendly — short paragraphs (2-4 sentences), no walls of text
- [ ] Proper heading hierarchy — H1 → H2 → H3, no skipping
- [ ] No duplicate content — every page says something unique that no other page says
- [ ] Specific over generic — name real places, real prices, real processes
- [ ] FAQ answers are complete sentences — never end with a colon
