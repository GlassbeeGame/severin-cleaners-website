# The Local Domination Blueprint

> **Historical reference.** This was the original strategic blueprint for building the Severin Hauling website from scratch. The phased approach and execution rhythm have been incorporated into the project's operational workflow. Keep this as a reference for the original strategic thinking.

> **Active operational workflow lives in**: `architecture/seo-standards.md` (operational workflows section)

---

## How to Outrank Your Competition Using AI

*For service businesses ready to go from invisible to inevitable*

---

## The Goal

This blueprint helps you work toward:
- Building a website that competes with established competitors
- Generating qualified leads consistently
- Improving your search visibility dramatically

---

## PHASE 1: THE FOUNDATION
*From zero to live website*

### Step 1: Account Setup
- Claude Code (AI developer) — $20/month
- GitHub (code storage) — free
- Vercel (website host) — free

### Step 2: Launch Your Website
Tell Claude exactly this:
"I need a website for my [service type] business in [city]. Create a website with Homepage, Services, Locations, About, Contact pages."

---

## PHASE 2: INTELLIGENCE GATHERING
*Know your market inside and out*

### Competitor Analysis
Open 10 competitor websites and analyze:
- What they're doing wrong
- What keywords they're missing
- How we can beat them

Document:
- Who ranks #1-10 for your main service
- Their Google review counts
- Their website weaknesses
- Their pricing (if shown)
- What customers complain about

### Keyword Research
Generate 100 keywords including:
- Emergency searches
- Problem-based searches
- Service-specific searches
- Location combinations
- Commercial intent keywords

Organize by difficulty, commercial value, and target page.

### Customer Intelligence
Read EVERY review of your top 5 competitors.
Document:
- Top 5 complaints (your opportunities)
- Top 5 praise points (must match these)
- Services they don't offer
- Response time issues
- Pricing complaints

---

## PHASE 3: BUILD YOUR ADVANTAGE
*Create the foundation for dominance*

### Technical SEO Foundation
- Complete schema markup with service catalog
- XML sitemap with proper priorities
- robots.txt optimized for crawling
- Canonical URLs for all pages
- Meta titles and descriptions for all pages

### Speed Optimization
- WebP format for all images
- Lazy loading for images
- Critical CSS for above-fold content
- Core Web Vitals optimization
- Fast mobile load times

### Content Creation

#### Service Pages (800-1,200 words each)
For each service include:
- What's included (detailed list)
- Common problems we solve
- Our process (step by step)
- Why choose us
- FAQs
- Emergency availability
- Service area details

#### Location Pages (1,000+ words each)
For each location include:
- Local service area details
- Landmarks and geography
- Local weather challenges
- Major employers served
- Neighborhood-specific content
- Response times for different areas

---

## PHASE 4: CONVERSION OPTIMIZATION
*Turn visitors into customers*

### Trust Signals
Add to every page:
- License numbers
- Insurance information
- Years in business
- Certifications
- Service guarantees
- Response time promises

### Lead Capture
- Sticky header with phone number
- Click-to-call buttons
- Contact form on every page
- Emergency hotline banner
- 'Get Quote' buttons

---

## PHASE 5: GROWTH ACCELERATION
*Scale what's working*

### Content Marketing
Write blog posts (1,500 words each):
1. "What to Do When [Common Problem]"
2. "Complete Guide to [Service] in [City]"
3. "How Much Does [Service] Cost in [Area]"
4. "[Season] [Service] Preparation Guide"
5. "Emergency [Service]: What You Need to Know"

### Local Citations
Submit business to: Google Business Profile, Bing Places, Apple Maps, Yelp, Facebook, industry directories.

### Geographic Expansion
Create pages for surrounding cities — each needs 1,000 words of unique local content.

---

## PHASE 6: DOMINATION
*Become the market leader*

### Service Expansion
Create pages for related services and connect with internal links.

### Continuous Improvement
- Fix 404 errors
- Improve low-performing pages
- Target new keywords
- Expand successful content

---

## Execution Rhythm

### Weekly Tasks:

#### Content Day (2 hours):
- Write a blog post about [topic]
- Create a new service page for [service]
- Expand a location page

#### Optimization Day (1 hour):
- Check for SEO issues
- Improve page load speed
- Fix broken links
- Update meta descriptions

#### Analysis Day (1 hour):
- What new content did competitors publish?
- What keywords are we missing?
- What can we do better?

---

## Quick Win Protocols

### "I Need Leads Fast"
1. Create emergency/urgent pages
2. Share in local Facebook groups
3. Update Google Business Profile
4. Reach out to past customers
5. Offer immediate service

### "Competitor Launched New Site"
1. Analyze their entire site
2. Identify what works
3. Improve on their approach
4. Add what they missed
5. Execute faster than them

### "Rankings Aren't Improving"
1. Check Google Search Console
2. Fix technical issues
3. Expand existing content
4. Build more links
5. Be patient — SEO compounds

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Start with Technical SEO — fix the foundation before adding content
2. Schema is Critical — use the most specific schema types available
3. Content Depth Matters — 1,000+ words for important pages
4. Local Content Must Be Unique — generic location pages don't rank
5. Internal Links Build Authority — connect related content strategically
6. Mobile Speed is Non-Negotiable — optimize for Core Web Vitals
7. Trust and Transparency Win — focus on user experience over keyword stuffing
